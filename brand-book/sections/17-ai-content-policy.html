Section 17

AI Content Policy

How NICHE uses AI in brand production — and where the line is.

Overview

Why this section exists in 2026

As of 2026, AI-generated content is ubiquitous and increasingly undetectable. Without explicit policy, brand teams default to what is convenient — which means AI voices, AI faces, and AI copy that doesn't sound like NICHE. This policy is proactive, not reactive.

Approved AI uses

Approved AI uses

Research and brief drafting. First-draft copywriting (human-reviewed and rewritten to NICHE voice before publication). Color palette exploration. Layout iteration. Reference image generation (not final production). Copy translation (human proofread after).

Prohibited uses

Prohibited uses

Synthetic human faces in any published NICHE material. AI-generated testimonials or student quotes. Final-draft official communications without human review. AI voice acting for NICHE brand narration. Replacing commissioned photography with AI imagery.

Brand voice prompting guide

Brand voice prompting guide

When using AI for copy drafts, include the following context in your prompt: provide the voice corpus from §8, the four voice principles, and three examples from the Do/Don't pairs. Specify: 'Do not use the following words: [forbidden words list from §8]'.

Human review triggers

Human review triggers

Any AI-generated content that will appear on: the NICHE website, IG feed, printed materials, event signage, or any public-facing platform must be reviewed by the brand owner or a designated brand steward before publication.

Pending Content

Brand owner input required.

Policy owner: Name a specific person as the AI content policy owner within NICHE. Without a named owner, the policy will not be enforced.

Tool list: Publish the approved tool list with version numbers. As of 2026, this list should include: Claude (Anthropic) for copy drafts, Midjourney / Stable Diffusion for reference imagery, CapCut for social video (with human script and human face only).
