Section 08

Voice, Tone & Messaging

"Find your niche" is three words. Write like that. Challenge the system, not the person. Side with the independent. Always.

Four Voice Principles

The rules behind the voice.

These principles are not aspirational guidelines. They are filters. Before publishing any copy, test it against all four. If it fails one, rewrite it.

1
Challenge the assumption, not the person.

NICHE questions the system ("wrong way?"), not the learner. Provocation is the register — but the target is always a belief or a norm, never the individual in front of you. The tone is confident, not condescending. Challengeable, not combative.

"Are you learning wrong way?" — not "You are learning wrong."
2
Short sentences earn their weight.

"Find your niche" is three words. Every word in a NICHE sentence is working. If a sentence doesn't tighten when you remove a word, you haven't removed enough words yet. Editorial precision, not casual informality.

"The photography program that teaches you to sell." — not "Our comprehensive photography program will teach you how to price, position, and sell your photographic work to clients."
3
Side with independents.

Use "you" / "your". Never "students" or "learners" — they flatten people into roles. Prefer "creators", "makers", "you". NICHE talks to the person who works independently, thinks independently, and earns independently. The copy reflects that respect.

"All Eyes on Independents." — not "All Eyes on Our Students."
4
Confident specificity over inspiring vagueness.

Vague inspiration is what every other institution sells. NICHE sells specificity. Numbers, outcomes, time frames. "Learn portfolio pricing in 4 hours" is more inspiring than "unlock your potential" — because it is believable. Belief precedes desire.

"4 weeks. 1 mentor. 1 working portfolio." — not "A transformative creative journey."

Forbidden Words

These words undo everything.

These words appear in the copy of every institution NICHE is positioning against. Using them signals conformity, not independence. Flag and replace on sight.

Empower NGO register. Implies the audience lacks power and you're granting it. NICHE's audience already has power — it needs direction, not permission.
Journey Travel metaphor overused by every bootcamp. Implies meandering. NICHE is specific and time-bounded: 11–30 November. That's not a journey.
Transformative What transformation claims, specificity proves. Write the outcome, not the adjective for the experience.
Passionate Self-declared passion is a red flag. NICHE assumes passion exists. The question is whether it's combined with judgment and craft.
Innovative Every institution claims it. None define it. If NICHE is innovative, the curriculum is the evidence — not the adjective.
Community Acceptable in alumni context only. "Join our community" as a call to action is the phonetic equivalent of a waiting room poster. Replace with specifics: "11–30 November, 40 makers, 12 programs."
Students Too institutional. NICHE's participants are creators, makers, you. "Students" implies a power hierarchy that NICHE's voice actively rejects.
Holistic Wellness spa register. NICHE is a creative institute, not a retreat. Specificity is the register, not integrative wholeness.
World-class Self-assessed quality claims earn immediate skepticism. Let the output and the faculty prove it — don't announce it.
Tagline Rules

These three words are protected.

The taglines below are NICHE's highest-value copy assets. They are not decorative — they are the brand's positioning in compressed form. Rules for use below.

Find your niche.
"Find your niche" Primary tagline. Always lowercase except at start of sentence. Never "Find Your Niche" unless typographically required (title case on print cover).
"Are you learning wrong way?" Challenge hook. Use on OOH, billboard, poster. Not for email subject lines — too confrontational in push context. Use where attention is earned, not demanded.
"All Eyes on Independents" Program / cohort-level hook. Use for event materials, IG announcement posts. Not the primary tagline — secondary positioning.
Thai translation Not yet decided — all current brand materials use English only. Decision required before Thai-language campaign launch. See §1 Foundation for pending decision.
Punctuation Period after "Find your niche." — mirrors the "N." submark's declarative period. Never use exclamation marks in NICHE copy. Never.
