Section 18

Partnership & Co-Branding Protocol

How NICHE appears alongside other brands.

Overview

The co-branding problem

Partners arrive at deadline with their own brand guidelines, wrong file formats, and requests that compromise the NICHE mark. This section exists to give the NICHE brand steward a written policy to defend.

Hierarchy principle

Hierarchy principle

NICHE is always the lead brand in its own materials. Partner logos appear at 70% of the NICHE wordmark's visual weight. No partner logo should compete with the primary NICHE wordmark.

Clear-space between marks

Clear-space between marks

Minimum distance between NICHE wordmark and any partner logo: 1× the cap-height of the NICHE E (same as the logo's own clear-space rule). Both marks must have their own clear-space zones satisfied independently.

Approved placements

Approved placements

Footer of NICHE materials (preferred). Separate 'Partners' page or slide. Not in the hero area. Not on the cover of brochures or posters.

Logo formats required from partners

Logo formats required from partners

SVG or PDF vector file. Minimum 1000px wide PNG with transparent background. If partner cannot supply these, NICHE is not responsible for quality of their mark in NICHE materials.

Pending Content

Brand owner input required.

Partner approval process: Who approves a partner logo before it appears in NICHE materials? Name a steward and a response time (suggest: 48 hours).

Exclusivity clauses: Are any program categories partner-exclusive? (e.g., one tech sponsor per cohort). Define before the November 2026 cohort.
