Overview

Purpose

What does NICHE exist to do? Not the academic definition — the operational one. Every decision in this brand book flows from the answer to this question.

Three Pillars — 12 Programs

Three Pillars

Creativity / Humanity / Entrepreneurship. Each pillar contains four programs. The pillars are not equal in personality — Creativity is expressive, Humanity is relational, Entrepreneurship is operational. Together they define a whole maker: someone who creates with judgment, connects with humanity, and builds with independence.

Positioning

Positioning

NICHE is closer in spirit to Hyper Island, Berghs, and IDEO U than to mainstream universities. Peer-learning. Editorial energy. Anti-academic-emblem gravitas.

Find your niche — unpacked

Find your niche — unpacked

Three words. The entire positioning compressed. Not 'find your passion' (too aspirational). Not 'build your career' (too pragmatic). Find your niche: find the specific, defensible thing you do better than generalists.

Pending Content

Brand owner input required.

Mission Statement: The full official mission statement requires input from the brand owner and founding team. Provisional: NICHE exists to teach judgment, not trends — helping creators, makers, and entrepreneurs carve the specific niche only they can occupy.

Founding Story: Origin context: who started NICHE, when, why, and what problem it addresses. Required for §2 Brand Story.

Pillar definitions: Each of the three pillars (Creativity, Humanity, Entrepreneurship) needs a 1–2 sentence brand definition that explains why these three are chosen together and what the combination means.
