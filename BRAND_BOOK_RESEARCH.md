# NICHE — Brand Book & Logo Research

> **Project:** NICHE — *New Institute of Creativity, Humanity & Entrepreneurship*
> **Tagline:** *Find your niche*
> **Date:** 2026-05-06
> **Status:** Research synthesis (3 parallel research streams)
> **Audience:** Designer + brand owner — to drive Phase 2 brand book authoring and final logo selection.

This document consolidates three parallel research streams (brand-book structure, logo selection, typography/color/motion) into a single decision-ready brief for the NICHE brand. Every recommendation here is opinionated. Treat it as the working draft of the brand book's editorial backbone — not as neutral background reading.

| Decision | Recommendation |

- **Name:** NICHE → acronym for *New Institute of Creativity, Humanity & Entrepreneurship*

- **Voice register:** declarative, slightly provocative, anti-mainstream, sides with the independent ("Find your niche", "Are you learning the wrong way?", "All eyes on independents")
- **Touchpoints already drafted:** brochure · document/program sheet · billboard · signage · namecard · IG story · IG post · t-shirt · poster

This positions NICHE close in spirit to **Hyper Island, Berghs, IDEO U, Domestika, MIT Media Lab** rather than to mainstream universities. The brand book must reflect this — peer-learning and editorial energy, not academic-emblem gravitas.

## 2. Logo Decision: Why OPT 02

### 2.1 The 12 universal selection criteria (2026)

Apply these in order — failing early ones disqualifies fastest.

1. **Legibility at 16px / favicon size** — does the form survive avatar scale?
2. **Monochrome integrity** — flat black on white, white on black, both must work.
3. **Scalability across 5 orders of magnitude** — 16px → 200px → A4 → 1m exhibition → building face.
4. **Silhouette distinctiveness** — squint test against 10 competitor marks; reverse-image search on Google + TinEye.
5. **US + EU trademark availability** — preliminary USPTO TESS + EUIPO eSearch.
6. **Cultural neutrality** — colors/shapes/spacing across target markets (SEA, EU at minimum for an international institute).
7. **Motion readiness** — describe a single coherent motion behavior in one sentence.
8. **Physical reproduction** — embroidery (1mm minimum stroke at garment scale), embossing, foil, single-color print.
9. **Partnership lockup compatibility** — sits at visual parity beside both simple and complex partner marks.
10. **AI-generation paranoia (2026-specific)** — does an LLM image generator output something resembling this from a generic prompt? If yes, distinctiveness is at risk.
11. **10-year aging test** — what trope is carrying the personality? Will it still feel current in 2034?
12. **WCAG 2.2 contrast** — 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large, on every approved background.

### 2.2 Honest pros/cons of the 3 candidates

#### OPT 01 — Chamfered geometric blocky wordmark
**Pros:** structural strength · embroidery/embossing flawless · proprietary chamfered formal language (not a default sans) · scales to building-face signage.
**Cons:** reads "tech-sector / industrial" — clashes with the **Humanity** pillar · limited motion vocabulary · risks reading as engineering rather than creative.

#### OPT 02 — Rounded humanist sans, tilted N (RECOMMENDED)
**Pros:** strongest personality of the three · tilted N is a genuine mnemonic · humanist forms map to **Humanity** without being literal · approachable across audiences (students, parents, accreditors) · obvious motion behavior · proven 50-year longevity (Gill Sans 1928, Optima 1958 lineage) · pairs perfectly with the "N." submark.
**Cons:** rounded humanist is currently the dominant aesthetic (Figma, Linear, Notion) — counter-reaction risk in 2028–2030 · asymmetric C requires extra construction documentation · distinctiveness depends on the specific custom drawing.

#### OPT 03 — Thin art-deco display, circle-as-i
**Pros:** unmistakable register, maximum differentiation · circle-replacing-i is conceptually strong (the dot reads as "the niche") · beautiful at hero scale · attractor for creative talent.
**Cons:** **fails embroidery test** — thin strokes physically unrenderable below 3mm · **fails 16px test** — strokes vanish · **fails diploma reproduction** at 2cm height · fashion/boutique register conflicts with tech/entrepreneurship pillars · highly trend-coupled.

### 2.3 The systems answer (not "pick one, retire two")

Treat the three candidates as a **logo system with scoped roles**, not as competing options for one job:

- **Institutional backbone** → OPT 02 + "N." submark
- **NICHE Labs / Tech / AI sub-brand** → OPT 01 (its industrial authority is the right register where OPT 02's warmth would feel mismatched — hackathons, AI partnerships, technical-program merch)
- **NICHE Journal / Annual editorial masthead** → OPT 03 (publication-mark longevity is shorter than institution-mark longevity; an annual cycle suits its trend-coupled aesthetic)

This gives NICHE three voices in one institution, each with correct scope, none redundant.

### 2.4 The "N." submark — protect the period

The period after N is doing genuine concept work: a **niche** is a carved recess, the period punctuates that claim. It also reads as a confident declarative sentence-ender — *"NICHE."* as a statement. Promote the "N." mark to system-canonical with its own clear-space and minimum-size documentation. It will live more than the wordmark will (favicon, app icon, IG avatar, embroidery, embossing).

## 3. Type System

### 3.1 Recommended pairing

| Role | Family | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Display / editorial** | **GT Sectra Display** (Grilli Type, commercial license) | Designed for *Reportagen* magazine. Sits exactly at calligraphic warmth × scalpel angularity — the register "Find your niche" at 120px belongs in. 30 styles across GT Sectra / Sectra Fine / Sectra Display. |
| **Body / UI / Thai** | **Bai Jamjuree** (Cadson Demak, free SIL OFL via Google Fonts) | Dual-script Thai + Latin in one file. Eurostyle-meets-superellipse — quiet, slightly technical, contrasts well with Sectra's warmth. Eliminates Thai font-fallback pain on a Bangkok project. |

**Why not Helvetica / Inter / Poppins:** Poppins is on every Thai creative-school site. Bai Jamjuree reads as more considered and locally authored.

**Variable font upgrade path:** Bai Jamjuree is not currently variable. Monitor **Futura 100** (multi-script, Thai planned 2026) if variable becomes a hard requirement.

## 4. Color System

### 4.1 WCAG 2.2 AA audit (computed)

| Pair | Ratio | AA Normal | AA Large |
|---|---|---|---|
| `#f2f1f0` ↔ `#252026` | 14.18:1 | ✅ | ✅ |
| `#cccecd` on `#252026` | 10.11:1 | ✅ | ✅ |
| `#fe1d25` on `#252026` | 4.12:1 | ❌ | ✅ |
| `#fe1d25` on `#f2f1f0` | 3.44:1 | ❌ | ✅ |
| `#779152` on `#252026` | 4.54:1 | ✅ (barely) | ✅ |
| `#779152` on `#f2f1f0` | 3.13:1 | ❌ | ✅ |
| `#cccecd` on `#f2f1f0` | 1.40:1 | ❌ | ❌ |

### 4.2 Hard rules (must appear in brand book)

- **`#fe1d25` red** — display only (≥18pt regular or ≥14pt bold). **Never** a hyperlink color on light backgrounds. **Never** body text.
- **`#cccecd` warm gray** — decorative, dividers, disabled states only. **Never** text, on either background.
- **`#779152` moss green** — body text allowed only on `#252026`. On `#f2f1f0`, large display only.

### 4.3 Tokenization (semantic layer)

```
color/surface/base                = #f2f1f0   page background
color/surface/ink                 = #252026   primary text, dark fills
color/surface/muted               = #cccecd   dividers/disabled — NO text
color/accent/brand                = #fe1d25   display, CTAs, icons — large text only
color/accent/secondary            = #779152   program signage — body on ink only
color/accent/secondary-shade      = #4a5c33   accessible green for body on light bg
color/accent/secondary-tint       = #b4c49a   decorative only — NO text
```

### 4.4 Should the palette grow?

**Hold at 5.** The tension between near-black, vivid red, and muted green *is* the identity. A 6th color dilutes that. Expand the green into a tints/shades scale (3 steps) so program tags and category chips can use accessible variants without breaking the system.

### 4.5 Dark-mode requirement

The current palette was authored in light-mode context. The brand book must publish explicit dark-mode token alternates — `#252026` does not auto-translate to system dark mode without contrast loss. WCAG requires 4.5:1 body text regardless of mode.

## 5. Photography & Art Direction

Closest published references: **Bloomberg Businessweek** (tension/harmony between images, hire photographers with personal POV) and **Hyper Island** (unpolished candid process shots, not aspirational product photography).

### 5.1 Do / Don't

**Do**
- Natural window light or single hard source — avoid 3-point studio polish.
- Subjects mid-task: hands annotating, laptop beside coffee, in flow.
- Slightly overexposed matte grade — keeps the off-white `#f2f1f0` skin alive in image tones.
- Negative space upper-third or left-third for type overlays.
- Mix close-detail (tools, textures) with environmental (workspace, room) at 2:1.
- Include physical making artifacts: sketchbooks, sticky notes, printed mockups.

**Don't**
- Desaturated moody dark filters — overused by bootcamp brands.
- Diversity-stock grids of people posing collaboratively.
- Render-only hero images without human presence.
- Warm orange Instagram filters — clash with cool near-black + moss green.
- Motion blur for drama — motion language handles kinetics; stills are anchored.

### 5.2 Color grade recipe (Lightroom / Camera Raw)

```
Exposure        +0.30
Highlights      −35
Shadows         +15
Blacks          +20  (matte lift)
Whites          −10
Temperature     +200K  (warm shift)
Vibrance        −10  (keeps reds from flaring)
Tone Curve      lift black point to 15/255
```

## 6. Motion System

Authority: IBM Carbon Motion. Five tokens cover everything NICHE will need.

| Token | Duration | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `motion/duration/micro` | 80ms | Icon state changes, checkbox toggles, hover fills |
| `motion/duration/quick` | 150ms | Button press, tooltip appear, tag hover |
| `motion/duration/base` | 280ms | Card entrance, drawer open, modal appear |
| `motion/duration/slow` | 500ms | Page transitions, hero text reveals |
| `motion/duration/logo` | 900ms | Logo reveal — staged: mark 0–400ms, wordmark 350–700ms, tagline 650–900ms |

### Easing

- Entrance: `cubic-bezier(0, 0, 0.3, 1)`
- Exit: `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0.14, 1, 1)`
- Standard (in-place): `cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0.38, 0.9)`

### NICHE-specific motion principles

1. **Type-first reveal** — display text enters word-by-word (not letter-by-letter — avoid the startup cliché). 40ms stagger per word. Sectra deserves to be *read*, not animated.
2. **Red activates on intent** — `#fe1d25` appears in motion only on hover/active states. It punctuates; it never auto-decorates on scroll.
3. **Program cards** — on hover, background cross-fades `#f2f1f0` → `#252026` at `quick` (150ms); category tag scales 1.0 → 1.05 simultaneously.

## 7. Voice & Tone

### 7.1 Four voice principles

1. **Challenge the assumption, not the person.** NICHE questions the system ("wrong way?"), not the learner. Provocation, never condescension.
2. **Short sentences earn their weight.** "Find your niche" is three words. Never expand what can be compressed.
3. **Side with independents.** Use "you" / "your". Never "students" or "learners" — they flatten people into roles. Prefer "creators", "makers", "you".
4. **Confident specificity over inspiring vagueness.** Not "unlock your potential" — "learn portfolio pricing in 4 hours."

### 7.2 Do / Don't pairs

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| The photography program that teaches you to sell. | Unlock your creative potential through transformative photography experiences. |
| Most schools teach trends. We teach judgment. | We believe in empowering the next generation of creative professionals. |
| Are you learning the wrong way? | Discover a better approach to creative learning today. |

## 8. Recommended Brand Book TOC (20 sections)

Tailored for a creative institute with 3 pillars, 12 programs, mixed photography, social-heavy presence, Bangkok-based with international reach.

1. **Brand Foundation** — mission, "Find your niche", three pillar definitions
2. **Brand Story** — origin, positioning, what NICHE is *not*
3. **Logo System** — primary wordmark (OPT 02), "N." submark, OPT 01/03 sub-brand roles, clear-space, minimum sizes, colorways
4. **Pillar & Program Lock-Ups** — hierarchy diagram: NICHE → Pillar → Program; lock-up grid for all 12 programs
5. **Color Palette** — 5 colors + green scale; WCAG table for every text-on-bg pair; dark-mode alternates
6. **Typography** — GT Sectra + Bai Jamjuree; print + screen variants; Thai/Latin handling
7. **Photography Direction** — three modes (Editorial faculty / Lifestyle studio / Student-Work documentation); mood boards per mode
8. **Voice, Tone & Messaging** — voice pillars, channel-tone matrix, forbidden words, tagline rules
9. **Iconography & Graphic Language** — icon system, mixed-photography collage technique, poster compositional principles
10. **Motion & Animation System** — 5 motion tokens, logo reveal spec, IG story transitions, prohibited effects
11. **Digital & Social Templates** — IG post/story safe zones, content pillars per program, email signature, LinkedIn banner
12. **Print & Physical Touchpoints** — brochure grid, billboard/signage spec, namecard, t-shirt placement, poster system
13. **Course Card & Certificate System** — repeatable course-card template (digital + print + IG); certificate template; micro-credential badge
14. **Faculty, Staff & Alumni Templates** — headshot crop ratios, bio short/long, "NICHE Alumni" badge, UGC repost rules
15. **Event & Wayfinding** — stage backdrop, pull-up banner, badge/lanyard, environmental signage scale guide
16. **Accessibility Standards** — WCAG 2.2 AA table, alt-text writing guide, no-flash + motion-safe rules, IG alt-text instructions
17. **AI Content Policy** — approved tools, mandatory human-review triggers, prohibited uses (synthetic faces, fake testimonials, final official comms), brand-voice prompting guide
18. **Partnership & Co-Branding Protocol** — co-logo grids, minimum clear-space, sponsor credit hierarchy
19. **Asset Library & File Naming** — Figma source, logo package, font licenses, photo library; naming conventions
20. **Governance & Version Log** — brand steward, approval process, version history, review cadence

## 10. What's Typically Missing in v1 Brand Books — Don't Skip These

These cause real operational pain at 6–18 months post-launch:

- **Motion / animation system** — without it, every video editor improvises.
- **AI-generated content rules** — vague "feel warm" instructions don't survive LLM workflows. Reference Purdue's 2024 policy.
- **Accessibility / WCAG** — color contrast is the #1 web a11y violation (83.6% of sites fail, WebAIM 2024). NICHE's red and gray fail body-text contrast — must be documented.
- **Dark-mode specs** — brand built in light mode doesn't auto-translate.
- **Cultural localization** — does "Find your niche" translate to Thai? Or stay English? Document the choice.
- **Partnership / co-branding** — sponsor materials arrive at deadline with bad lockups; without rules, no one has authority to reject.
- **Governance** — who approves new applications? What triggers a brand review? Name a steward and a cadence.

## 11. Format & Delivery

- **Primary:** web-hosted brand portal (Frontify / Zeroheight / custom Webflow/Notion) — searchable, version-controlled, vendor-shareable without file transfer.
- **Source of truth:** Figma file with component library + brand tokens (Variables) — designers pull directly.
- **Vendor / archive:** PDF export, 60–80 pages, generated on demand from the portal — not maintained as primary.
- **Templates:** Canva or Figma Community file — for non-designers producing social/event content.

**Do not ship a static PDF as the only brand book.** That's the 2010s standard and it ages out within a year of launch.
